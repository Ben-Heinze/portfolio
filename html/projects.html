Software Engineering

HRDC Maintenance Request Form
  • Goal: The Human Resource Development Council (HRDC) in Bozeman asked us to create a low-cost, robust, and easily-manageable maintenance request system for their organization.
  • Method: Using React and EmailJS, my scrum team developed modern and responsive application to notify their maintenance team whenever someone sends a request while emphasizing on accessibility and simplicity.
  • Outcome: Delivered a free, accessible, and easily manageable React application. Each competitor offered expensive solutions ranging from $100-500 solutions. My team kept the non-profit's inexpensive request and delivered on that promise.

Diction Analysis of Papers from the Journal of ACM

  • Goal: To investigate whether the integration of Large Language Models (LLMs) in academic writing has influenced the common diction of adjectives and verbs used in free academic papers in the Journal of ACM from 2018 to 2024.
  • Method: The project involved preprocessing text data through tokenization, parts-of-speech tagging, stemming, and normalizing word frequencies to reduce bias from larger papers. The analysis focused on detecting shifts in the usage of adjectives and verbs over the given time period.
  • Outcome: The outcome of the study was inconclusive, indicating that more research with an expanded dataset is needed to draw more definitive conclusions, particularly due to the influence of the 2020 pandemic and the recency of LLMs.

UNHCR Refugee Data Visualization
  • Goal: To explore five questions of interest related to international disputes using the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) dataset from 2010-2022.
  • Method: The project involved creating two trend graphs, three geospatial graphs, a bar chart, and a line chart to visualize the data. These visualizations were used to analyze and make inferences about the patterns in international disputes.
  • Outcome: The project successfully identified significant factors that explained the patterns observed in the visualizations. The findings were documented in a full paper.

Artificial Intelligence

Bayesian Networks
  • Goal: To analyze and compare the efficiency and accuracy of Variable Elimination and Gibbs Sampling algorithms, determining whether the trade-offs in computational efficiency of Variable Elimination are justified by its accuracy across different network scales, and to evaluate if Gibbs Sampling can serve as a viable alternative.
  • Method: The project involved hand-coding the Variable Elimination and Gibbs Sampling algorithms and testing them under various levels of evidence (none, little, and moderate) on different network sizes, including the ALARM network.
  • Outcome: The study provided insights into the relative strengths and limitations of the two algorithms, assessing their performance in terms of efficiency and accuracy across different scenarios and network sizes.

Sudoku Solver
  • Goal: To analyze and compare the effectiveness of various search algorithms in solving Sudoku puzzles of differing difficulty levels.
  • Method: The project involved hand-coding multiple algorithms, including backtracking, backtracking with forward search, backtracking with arc consistency, simulated annealing, and genetic algorithms. These algorithms were tested on Sudoku puzzles ranging from easy to evil difficulty levels. The number of decisions made and the fitness score at each decision point were measured for comparison.
  • Outcome: The study provided a comparative analysis of the performance of different search algorithms in solving Sudoku puzzles, highlighting the strengths and weaknesses of each algorithm based on the number of decisions made and fitness scores.

Catscript Compiler

  • Goal: To handcode a compiler to a scripting language called Catscript.
  • Method: The project utilized recursive descent to implement the tokenizer, parser, and bytecode generator. The development process was graded through test-driven development written by peers. The language handles type inference, objects, primitives, and other capabilities.
  • Outcome: The project successfully resulted in a working implementation of the Catscript compiler, capable of generating JVM Bytecode. The implementation was thoroughly tested and documented.
