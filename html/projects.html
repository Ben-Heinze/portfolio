UNHCR Refugee Data Visualization

Using the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) dataset from 2010-2022, we explored five questions of interest and sought inferences through data visualization. To achieve this, we created two trend graphs, three geospatial graphs, a bar chart, and a line chart. These visualizations were used to examine international disputes, and we successfully identified significant factors that explained the patterns observed in the graphs. Diction Analysis of Papers from the Journal of ACM

Investigated whether the integration of LLMs in academic writing has shifted common diction of adjectives and verbs used in free academic papers in the Journal of ACM from 2028-2024. Preprocessing consisted of tokenization, parts-of-speech tagging, stemming, and normalizing the frequencies to remove bias from larger papers. Further investigation that expands the scope of our dataset is necessary since the 2020 pandemic and the recency of LLMs dramatically skewed the outcomes.

Bayesian Networks

Analyzed the relative strengths and limitations of Variable Elimination and Gibbs Sampling algorithms in terms of efficiency and accuracy by hand-coding them directly. The study investigates whether the trade-offs in computational efficiency inherent to Variable Elimination are justified by its accuracy across various network scales. It also considers if Gibbs Sampling can serve as an alternative. We tested various levels of evidence: none, little, and moderate on various network sizes. The image to the left represents the ALARM network which was tested in the study.

Sudoku Solver

This paper presents our group's analysis of using various search algorithms to attempt to find Sudoku solutions. we hand-coded backtracking, backtracking with forward search, backtracking with arc consistency, simulated annealing, and genetic algorithms to solve Sudoku puzzles of differing difficulty levels. Through experimentation, we implemented each algorithm to solve puzzles ranging from easy to evil difficulty. We measured both the number of decisions made and the fitness score at each decision point to use for comparison.

Catscript Compiler

Implementing the tokenizer, parser, and bytecode generator to compile Catscript was my college capstone which was graded through test-driven development. At its core, Catscript is a statically typed programming language designed to compile into JVM Bytecode. It utilizes the recursive descent algorithm and handles type inference, objects, primitives, among other capabilities highlighted in this documentation.
