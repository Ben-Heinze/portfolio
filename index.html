About me

I'm Ben Heinze, a computer science graduate from Montana State University. Moving around most of my early childhood due to my father's military service, my family eventually settled in Helena, Montana in 2011. I stayed in Helena until I graduated from Capital High School in 2019, then sought a film degree from Montana State University Bozeman. After two full years in the program, I decided to switch majors to computer science.

Graduating from Montana State University in 2024, I'm versed in many sectors of computer science (databases, artificial intelligence, web development). I aim to specialize in software engineering and data analysis. Despite having no professional experience in software engineering, I've worked in a four-month scrum team with the goal of giving a complete application to Bozeman MT's Human Resource Development Council (HRDC). I have a strong background in working well within teams and was the only recipient of the Outstanding Undergraduate Course Assistant, awarded by MSU's computer science director, John Paxton.
